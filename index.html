Selected work

Three projects that shaped how I think about language in products.

01
Conversational AI · Microcopy · Voice & Tone

Navigator — Voice for an AI assistant

Staffbase SE · Berlin · 2024–present

The challenge

Navigator is a conversational AI assistant embedded in an enterprise internal comms platform used by tens of thousands of employees. Writing for it meant finding a voice that feels trustworthy without being robotic, and clear without being oversimplified — across a user base that ranges from office staff to frontline workers.

My approach

I defined the voice and tone framework for Navigator from the ground up, including how it handles uncertainty, partial answers, and handoffs to a human. Then I wrote and iterated on all conversational states — greetings, clarifications, errors, and fallbacks — working closely with Product, Design, and Engineering.

"Microcopy for AI assistants is mostly edge cases. The happy path takes care of itself. My job was making sure that when Navigator doesn't know something, it says so honestly and still moves the user forward."

02
Tool Design · Localisation · AI Prototyping

Figma CAT-tool plugin — bridging design and translation

Master's thesis project · Universität Potsdam · 2024

The challenge

UX writers and translators work in fundamentally different environments. Content created in Figma loses its structure the moment it enters a CAT tool: strings get stripped of context, translators can't see how text fits in the interface, and the resulting handoff wastes time on both sides.

My approach

I prototyped a Figma plugin that bridges the two workflows: extracting strings with design context intact, passing them to a translation memory environment, and reimporting translated text in the correct layers. The project combined my background in CAT tools (SDL Trados, MemoQ) with hands-on AI prototyping.

"This project started from a real frustration I had every week at work. Writing copy in Figma and then constantly going to other places to check for consistency with other copy, making sure we're using the right terminology and following the styleguide, felt like a problem nobody had bothered to solve yet. I built a version that did."

03
Content Design · AI Enablement · Workshop

AI literacy workshop for linguists — making tools approachable

Universität Potsdam · 2025

The challenge

Linguistics students at the University of Potsdam were curious about AI tools but had no practical framework for using them thoughtfully. The audience ranged from students with strong technical instincts to those with none but I believe all of them learned something valuable they can use immediately.

My approach

I designed and delivered a hands-on workshop introducing prompt engineering and practical AI tool-building. The curriculum moved from concept to application in a single session: first establishing why language matters in AI interactions, then having participants write and iterate on prompts in real time.

"Teaching something you know well to people who are new to it is its own kind of UX writing problem. The content has to meet the user where they are, not where you'd like them to be. That meant rebuilding my own assumptions from the ground up."
